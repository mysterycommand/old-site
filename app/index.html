The Nerdery & Top Secret Partner Z

  • HTML5

Top Secret Partner Z developed a proprietary algorithm for analysing social networks. With potential applications for everything from terrorist threat assesment, to identifying key influencers for advertising and marketing opportunities, to real-time political campaign effectiveness analytics, Top Secret Partner Z needed a client-side application to make harnessing the power of their engineering accomplishments both accessible and intuitive. Working with 3 designers, 2 .NET developers, and 4 front-end developers, my primary responsibility was integrating Backbone views and models with D3.js and jQuery UI components to generate an interactive force-directed network graph of key actors and relationships identified by Top Secret Partner Z's algorithm. SAG-AFTRAnumbers.com January 2012 - Present

SAG-AFTRA Twin Cities

  • H5BP
  • CoffeeScript
  • RequireJS
  • jQuery
  • TangleKit
  • Chosen
  • Sass

This online tool built for the Twin Cities chapter of AFTRA (now SAG-AFTRA) allows users to estimate the cost of hiring union talent for their next television or radio production. The interface builds on a story-problem metaphor, allowing the user to simply "complete the sentence" and thereby create a line-item estimate of the various union costs and fees associated with their project, and ultimately arriving at a calculated "grand total" estimate. I worked on this project with fellow freelancer and designer Liina Lundin. I did all the development myself use the then-new-to-me tools: CoffeeScript, RequireJS, and TangleKit. It's been a fun and challenging project. It is currently in the final review stage, and will be launching very soon.

BanyanRFP.com March 2011 - December 2011

ArcStone & BanyanRFP

  • Backbone
  • jQuery
  • jQuery UI
  • jQuery Templates
  • Google Closure Library

After selling his share of his previous company, Dave Sampsell was looking to re-invest some of his profits in a new venture. ArcStone won the project and hired me to help build out the front-end of an application that allows legal firms to quickly scaffold and customize requests for proposals for legal work. Working with ArcStone's team, we built the application on top of the Symfony 2 PHP Framework on the server and Backbone on the client. This was my first project of this scale, and while Backbone was a huge help, it also took a lot of getting used to. Due to the nature of the application I found myself struggling with deeply nested models and collections (to manage something akin to paragraphs, sub-paragraphs, and sections), as well as the nuances of high-performance functional programming (that is, efficiently passing partial function applications around as arguments). However, I'm proud to say that the site went live on time and on budget, is serving customers today, and has received resoundingly positive feedback.
